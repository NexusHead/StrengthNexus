Bodyweight Mastery

Where you can get strong anytime and anywhere.

Vertical Pull

Pull-Up Progressions
1. Dead Hang – Build grip and shoulder strength by hanging from a bar.
2. Scapular Pulls – Engage scapula to initiate pulling movement.
3. Negative Pull-Ups – Control descent to build eccentric strength.
4. Band-Assisted Pull-Ups – Use resistance bands to work full ROM.
5. Jumping Pull-Ups – Explode upward with legs, lower under control.
6. Chin-Ups – Supinated grip, builds biceps & lats.
7. Full Pull-Ups – Overhand grip, control up and down.
8. Wide Pull-Ups – Increase lat activation and scapular control.
9. Archer Pull-Ups – Shift weight side to side for unilateral strength.
10. Typewriter Pull-Ups – Dynamic lateral pulls across the bar.

Horizontal Pull

Bodyweight Row Progressions
1. Wall Rows – Use a wall for support and scapula activation.
2. Table Rows – Under a desk/table, pull chest to edge.
3. Low Bar Rows – Horizontal body under low bar, pull chest to bar.
4. Feet-Elevated Rows – Increase resistance via incline.
5. One-Leg Rows – Adds instability for core and unilateral focus.
6. Weighted Rows – Add backpack weight for intensity.
7. Towel Rows – Use towels for improved grip.
8. Ring/TRX Rows – Develops control and stability.
9. Archer Rows – Single-arm focused row with body shift.
10. One-Arm Rows – Ultimate unilateral bodyweight pull strength.

Vertical Push

Handstand Push-Up Progressions
1. Pike Holds – Head down, hips up on floor, mimics vertical pressing.
2. Wall Pike Push-Ups – Build shoulder strength at an incline.
3. Elevated Pike Push-Ups – Feet on object to increase load.
4. Headstand Holds – Wall-supported balance practice.
5. Wall Walks – Build coordination walking up wall.
6. Wall Handstand Holds – Lockout strength for handstand.
7. Partial HSPUs – Limited ROM reps to develop strength.
8. Wall HSPUs – Full range wall-supported push-ups.
9. Freestanding Negatives – Control eccentric without wall.
10. Freestanding HSPUs – Elite vertical pressing control and balance.

Horizontal Push

Push-Up Progressions
1. Wall Push-Ups – Beginner movement for chest and triceps.
2. Knee Push-Ups – Modified full push-up with knee support.
3. Incline Push-Ups – Use bench to reduce load.
4. Standard Push-Ups – Full-body tension, elbow control.
5. Diamond Push-Ups – Triceps-focused narrow grip.
6. Wide Push-Ups – Emphasize chest stretch and contraction.
7. Decline Push-Ups – Feet elevated for harder resistance.
8. Archer Push-Ups – Lateral chest and unilateral control.
9. Clap Push-Ups – Plyometric upper body explosion.
10. One-Arm Push-Ups – Master-level horizontal pushing strength.

Core

Core Progressions
1. Hollow Holds – Midline stability, core control.
2. Crunches – Activate upper abdominals.
3. Leg Raises – Target lower abs and hip flexors.
4. Reverse Crunches – Pelvic tilt and ab squeeze.
5. Planks – Full core and isometric endurance.
6. Side Planks – Target obliques and lateral line.
7. V-Ups – Total ab contraction with dynamic motion.
8. Hanging Knee Raises – Anti-swing core strength.
9. Toes to Bar – Full compression under control.
10. Dragon Flags – Advanced anti-extension ab strength.

Statics

Static Hold Mastery
1. Superman Hold – Posterior chain activation.
2. Hollow Body Hold – Total body tension.
3. Wall Handstand Hold – Shoulder and balance development.
4. Elbow Planche – Straight-arm strength on elbows.
5. Tuck Planche – Compact static strength.
6. Advanced Tuck Planche – Core and shoulder elevation.
7. Full Planche – Elite static strength and control.
8. Back Lever – Horizontal posterior chain static.
9. Front Lever – Lat and core tension horizontal hold.
10. Iron Flag – Side lever requiring immense shoulder and oblique strength.

Gear Recommendations

Pull-Up Bar – Foundation of all vertical pulling. Wall-mounted or door-frame types are ideal.
Rings – Versatile for push/pull and core training. Improves joint health and range of motion.
Resistance Bands – Useful for assisted reps and mobility work. Progressive overload and support.
Parallettes – Ideal for handstand push-ups, L-sits, planche work. Reduces wrist strain.
Weighted Vest – Increases load progressively once bodyweight movements become easy.
