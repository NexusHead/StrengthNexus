Pulling: The First Pillar

Pulling exercises are fundamental to bodyweight training, focusing on building strength in the back, biceps, and grip. These exercises are essential for developing the foundation needed for advanced calisthenics movements.

Exercises

Beginner

  • Assisted Pull-Ups: Use resistance bands or a partner to assist you. Focus on full range of motion.
  • Negative Pull-Ups: Jump to the top position and slowly lower yourself. Focus on the eccentric phase.
  • Inverted Rows: Perform rows with your body at an incline, feet on the ground.
  • Australian Pull-Ups: Perform rows with your body horizontal, feet elevated.
  • Scapular Pull-Ups: Focus on engaging the scapula without bending the elbows.

Intermediate

  • Pull-Ups: Master the full range of motion with an overhand grip.
  • Chin-Ups: Similar to pull-ups but with a supinated (underhand) grip.
  • Close-Grip Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups with a narrow grip to target different muscle groups.
  • Wide-Grip Pull-Ups: Use a wide grip to focus more on the lats.
  • L-Sit Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups while maintaining an L-sit position.

Advanced

  • Archer Pull-Ups: Pull with one arm while extending the other.
  • Typewriter Pull-Ups: Move horizontally across the bar while maintaining a pull-up position.
  • Muscle-Ups: Transition from a pull-up to a dip.
  • Front Lever Rows: Perform rows while maintaining a front lever position.
  • One-Arm Pull-Ups: The ultimate test of pulling strength.

Incredibly Advanced

  • Ice Cream Makers: Hang from the bar and rotate your body around it.
  • Front Lever Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups from a front lever position.
  • No-Hands Muscle-Ups: Transition from a pull-up to a dip without using hands.
  • Weighted Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups with added weight.
  • One-Arm Front Lever Pull-Ups: Advanced front lever pull-ups with one arm.

Champion

  • 1.5-2x Bodyweight Weighted Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups with significant added weight.
  • One-Arm Muscle-Ups: Transition from a one-arm pull-up to a dip.
  • Dynamic Front Lever Rows: Perform dynamic rows while maintaining a front lever position.
  • Full Planche Muscle-Ups: Transition from a planche position to a dip.
  • Explosive Muscle-Ups: Perform muscle-ups with explosive speed and power.

Pushing: The Second Pillar

Pushing exercises are crucial for developing upper body strength, focusing on the chest, shoulders, and triceps. These exercises are essential for balance and functional strength.

Exercises

Beginner

  • Incline Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your feet elevated.
  • Knee Push-Ups: Perform push-ups from your knees.
  • Wall Push-Ups: Perform push-ups against a wall.
  • Bench Dips: Perform dips with your feet on the ground.
  • Close-Grip Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with a narrow grip.

Intermediate

  • Push-Ups: Master the full range of motion with a standard grip.
  • Dips: Perform dips on parallel bars.
  • Decline Push-Ups: Elevate your feet higher than your hands.
  • Diamond Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your hands close together.
  • Hindu Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with a dynamic, flowing motion.

Advanced

  • Handstand Push-Ups: Perform push-ups in a handstand position against a wall.
  • Ring Dips: Perform dips on gymnastic rings for added instability.
  • Pseudo Planche Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your hands low and leaning forward.
  • Archer Push-Ups: Focus on unilateral strength by extending one arm.
  • Clap Push-Ups: Explode up and clap your hands together.

Incredibly Advanced

  • Planche Push-Ups: Perform push-ups in a planche position.
  • 90-Degree Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your body at a 90-degree angle.
  • One-Arm Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with one arm.
  • Weighted Dips: Perform dips with added weight.
  • Full Planche Dips: Perform dips in a full planche position.

Champion

  • 2x Bodyweight Weighted Dips: Perform dips with double your body weight.
  • One-Arm Planche Push-Ups: Perform push-ups in a planche position with one arm.
  • Dynamic Planche Push-Ups: Perform dynamic push-ups in a planche position.
  • Explosive 90-Degree Push-Ups: Perform explosive push-ups at a 90-degree angle.
  • Full Planche to Handstand Push-Ups: Transition from a planche to a handstand push-up.

Legs: The Third Pillar

Leg exercises are vital for overall strength and endurance. They focus on building power in the quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves, essential for functional movement and athletic performance.

Exercises

Beginner

  • Bodyweight Squats: Perform squats using only your body weight.
  • Lunges: Perform lunges to target each leg individually.
  • Step-Ups: Step up onto a raised platform.
  • Glute Bridges: Lie on your back and lift your hips.
  • Calf Raises: Stand on the edge of a step and raise your heels.

Intermediate

  • Jump Squats: Perform squats with a jump at the top.
  • Walking Lunges: Perform lunges while walking forward.
  • Bulgarian Split Squats: Perform single-leg squats with the rear foot elevated.
  • Pistol Squats: Perform single-leg squats with the non-working leg extended.
  • Donkey Kicks: Kick your legs back while in a push-up position.

Advanced

  • Box Jumps: Jump onto a raised platform.
  • Single-Leg Box Jumps: Jump onto a raised platform using one leg.
  • Depth Jumps: Jump down from a raised platform and immediately jump up.
  • Shrimp Squats: Perform squats while holding your feet.
