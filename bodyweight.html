Vertical Pull Progressions

Vertical pull exercises focus on building strength and endurance in the back, biceps, and grip. These progressions will help you develop the necessary strength for advanced movements like muscle-ups.

  • Assisted Pull-Ups: Use resistance bands or a partner to assist you. Perform 3 sets x 8-10 reps.
  • Negative Pull-Ups: Focus on the eccentric phase of the pull-up. Perform 3 sets x 5 reps.
  • Pull-Ups: Master the full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 reps.
  • Chin-Ups: Similar to pull-ups but with a supinated grip. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 reps.
  • Muscle-Up Progressions: Work on the transition from pull-up to dip. Perform 3 sets x 3-5 reps.

Horizontal Pull Progressions

Horizontal pull exercises target the back, rear delts, and biceps. These progressions will improve your pulling strength and posture.

  • Inverted Rows: Perform rows with your body at an incline. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps.
  • Ring Rows: Use gymnastic rings to add instability. Perform 3 sets x 8-12 reps.
  • One-Arm Rows: Focus on unilateral strength. Perform 3 sets x 6-8 reps per arm.
  • Australian Pull-Ups: Perform rows with your body horizontal. Perform 3 sets x 12-15 reps.

Vertical Push Progressions

Vertical push exercises focus on building strength in the shoulders, triceps, and chest. These progressions will help you develop the necessary strength for advanced movements like handstand push-ups.

  • Incline Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your feet elevated. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps.
  • Push-Ups: Master the full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 8-12 reps.
  • Handstand Push-Ups (against a wall): Work on the full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 3-5 reps.
  • Archer Push-Ups: Focus on unilateral strength. Perform 3 sets x 5-8 reps per arm.

Horizontal Push Progressions

Horizontal push exercises target the chest, shoulders, and triceps. These progressions will improve your pushing strength and stability.

  • Incline Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your feet elevated. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps.
  • Push-Ups: Master the full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 8-12 reps.
  • Decline Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your feet elevated higher than your hands. Perform 3 sets x 6-10 reps.
  • Clap Push-Ups: Focus on explosive strength. Perform 3 sets x 5-8 reps.

Core Progressions

Core exercises focus on building strength and endurance in the abdominals, lower back, and obliques. These progressions will improve your overall stability and control.

  • Plank: Hold a static position. Perform 3 sets x 30-60 seconds.
  • Side Plank: Hold a static position on each side. Perform 3 sets x 20-30 seconds each side.
  • Hanging Leg Raises: Focus on controlled movement. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 reps.
  • Dragon Flags: Advanced core exercise. Perform 3 sets x 3-5 reps.

Statics Progressions

Static exercises focus on building isometric strength and control. These progressions will improve your overall body awareness and stability.

  • Wall Sit: Hold a static position. Perform 3 sets x 30-60 seconds.
  • Planche Lean: Work on shoulder and core stability. Perform 3 sets x 10-20 seconds.
  • Front Lever Hold: Advanced static hold. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 seconds.
  • Back Lever Hold: Advanced static hold. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 seconds.

Gear Needed

For the workouts, you will need:

  • Pull-Up Bar: Essential for vertical pull exercises.
  • Rings (optional): Adds instability and challenge to exercises.
  • Yoga Mat: Provides comfort and support for floor exercises.
  • Resistance Bands (optional): Useful for assisted exercises and warm-ups.

Programs of Varying Difficulty

Choose a program based on your current fitness level and progress accordingly.

Beginner Program

Focus on building a solid foundation of strength and endurance.

  • Assisted Pull-Ups: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Incline Push-Ups: 3 sets x 10-15 reps
  • Plank: 3 sets x 30 seconds

Intermediate Program

Challenge yourself with more advanced exercises and higher intensity.

  • Pull-Ups: 3 sets x 5-10 reps
  • Push-Ups: 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  • Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets x 5-10 reps

Advanced Program

Push your limits with advanced movements and lower rep ranges.

  • Muscle-Up Progressions: 3 sets x 3-5 reps
  • Handstand Push-Ups: 3 sets x 3-5 reps
  • Dragon Flags: 3 sets x 3-5 reps
