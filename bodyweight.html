Vertical Pull Progressions

Vertical pull exercises focus on building strength and endurance in the back, biceps, and grip. These progressions will help you develop the necessary strength for advanced movements like one-arm pull-ups.

  • Assisted Pull-Ups: Use resistance bands or a partner to assist you. Focus on full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 8-10 reps.
  • Negative Pull-Ups: Jump to the top position and slowly lower yourself. Focus on the eccentric phase. Perform 3 sets x 5 reps.
  • Pull-Ups: Master the full range of motion with an overhand grip. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 reps.
  • Chin-Ups: Similar to pull-ups but with a supinated (underhand) grip. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 reps.
  • Close-Grip Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups with a narrow grip to target different muscle groups. Perform 3 sets x 6-8 reps.
  • Wide-Grip Pull-Ups: Use a wide grip to focus more on the lats. Perform 3 sets x 6-8 reps.
  • L-Sit Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups while maintaining an L-sit position. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • Archer Pull-Ups: Pull with one arm while extending the other. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps per arm.
  • Typewriter Pull-Ups: Move horizontally across the bar while maintaining a pull-up position. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • One-Arm Pull-Ups: The ultimate test of pulling strength. Perform 3 sets x 1-3 reps per arm.

Horizontal Pull Progressions

Horizontal pull exercises target the back, rear delts, and biceps. These progressions will improve your pulling strength and posture.

  • Inverted Rows: Perform rows with your body at an incline, feet on the ground. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps.
  • Feet-Elevated Inverted Rows: Increase the difficulty by elevating your feet. Perform 3 sets x 8-12 reps.
  • Ring Rows: Use gymnastic rings to add instability. Perform 3 sets x 8-12 reps.
  • One-Arm Rows: Focus on unilateral strength using a single ring or towel. Perform 3 sets x 6-8 reps per arm.
  • Australian Pull-Ups: Perform rows with your body horizontal, feet elevated. Perform 3 sets x 12-15 reps.
  • Archer Rows: Pull with one arm while extending the other. Perform 3 sets x 6-8 reps per arm.
  • Typewriter Rows: Move horizontally across the bar while maintaining a row position. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • Front Lever Rows: Perform rows while maintaining a front lever position. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • Rear-Delt Flyes with Rings: Focus on the rear delts by externally rotating the arms. Perform 3 sets x 8-10 reps.
  • Front Lever Pull-Ups: Perform pull-ups from a front lever position. Perform 3 sets x 3-5 reps.

Vertical Push Progressions

Vertical push exercises focus on building strength in the shoulders, triceps, and chest. These progressions will help you develop the necessary strength for advanced movements like planche push-ups.

  • Incline Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your feet elevated. Focus on full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps.
  • Push-Ups: Master the full range of motion with a standard grip. Perform 3 sets x 8-12 reps.
  • Diamond Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your hands close together. Perform 3 sets x 6-10 reps.
  • Decline Push-Ups: Elevate your feet higher than your hands. Perform 3 sets x 6-10 reps.
  • Pike Push-Ups: Keep your feet elevated and body in a pike position. Perform 3 sets x 5-8 reps.
  • Handstand Push-Ups (against a wall): Work on the full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 3-5 reps.
  • Archer Push-Ups: Focus on unilateral strength by extending one arm. Perform 3 sets x 5-8 reps per arm.
  • Pseudo Planche Push-Ups: Keep your hands low and lean forward. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • Tiger-Bend Push-Ups: Bend your arms in a tiger-bend position. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • Planche Push-Ups: The ultimate test of horizontal pushing strength. Perform 3 sets x 1-3 reps.

Horizontal Push Progressions

Horizontal push exercises target the chest, shoulders, and triceps. These progressions will improve your pushing strength and stability.

  • Incline Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your feet elevated. Focus on full range of motion. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps.
  • Push-Ups: Master the full range of motion with a standard grip. Perform 3 sets x 8-12 reps.
  • Wide-Grip Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with a wide grip to target the chest. Perform 3 sets x 6-10 reps.
  • Decline Push-Ups: Elevate your feet higher than your hands. Perform 3 sets x 6-10 reps.
  • Clap Push-Ups: Explode up and clap your hands together. Perform 3 sets x 5-8 reps.
  • Divebomber Push-Ups: Perform a dynamic push-up with a swooping motion. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • Pseudo Planche Push-Ups: Keep your hands low and lean forward. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • Maltese Push-Ups: Perform push-ups with your arms externally rotated. Perform 3 sets x 5-7 reps.
  • One-Arm Push-Ups: Focus on unilateral strength. Perform 3 sets x 3-5 reps per arm.
  • Freestanding 90-Degree Handstand Push-Ups: The ultimate test of vertical pushing strength. Perform 3 sets x 1-3 reps.

Core Progressions

Core exercises focus on building strength and endurance in the abdominals, lower back, and obliques. These progressions will improve your overall stability and control.

  • Plank: Hold a static position with your body in a straight line. Perform 3 sets x 30-60 seconds.
  • Side Plank: Hold a static position on each side. Perform 3 sets x 20-30 seconds each side.
  • Reverse Plank: Hold a static position facing upwards. Perform 3 sets x 20-30 seconds.
  • Bicycle Crunches: Perform a bicycle motion with your legs while crunching. Perform 3 sets x 15-20 reps per side.
  • Leg Raises: Lie on your back and raise your legs. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps.
  • Hanging Leg Raises: Hang from a bar and raise your legs. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 reps.
  • Russian Twists: Rotate your torso while holding a weight or medicine ball. Perform 3 sets x 15-20 reps per side.
  • Dragon Flags: Lie on your back and raise your body while keeping it straight. Perform 3 sets x 3-5 reps.
  • Windshield Wipers: Lie on your back and rotate your legs side to side. Perform 3 sets x 10-15 reps per side.
  • Front Lever and Full Planche: Choose one advanced static hold to master. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 seconds.

Statics Progressions

Static exercises focus on building isometric strength and control. These progressions will improve your overall body awareness and stability.

  • Wall Sit: Hold a static position with your back against a wall. Perform 3 sets x 30-60 seconds.
  • Planche Lean: Lean forward with your arms extended, keeping your body straight. Perform 3 sets x 10-20 seconds.
  • Tuck Planche: Hold a planche position with your knees tucked. Perform 3 sets x 10-20 seconds.
  • Advanced Tuck Planche: Hold a planche position with your knees slightly extended. Perform 3 sets x 10-20 seconds.
  • Straddle Planche: Hold a planche position with your legs straddled. Perform 3 sets x 10-20 seconds.
  • Full Planche: Hold a planche position with your legs fully extended. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 seconds.
  • Iron Cross: Hold a cross position with your arms extended. Perform 3 sets x 5-10 seconds.

Gear Needed

For the workouts, you will need:

  • Pull-Up Bar: Essential for vertical pull exercises. It allows you to perform a variety of pull-up variations and hang exercises.
  • Dip Bar: Useful for tricep dips and other bodyweight exercises that require parallel bars.
  • Weight Belt: Useful for adding resistance to bodyweight exercises like pull-ups and dips.
  • Resistance Bands: Useful for assisted exercises, warm-ups, and adding resistance to bodyweight movements.
  • Parallettes: Allows for a greater range of motion in exercises like L-sits and planche holds, improving wrist and shoulder strength.

Programs of Varying Difficulty

Choose a program based on your current fitness level and progress accordingly.

Full Body Program

Work your entire body with a mix of exercises. Perform each exercise for 3 sets with 1-2 minutes rest between sets.

  • Pull-Ups: 3 sets x 5-10 reps
  • Push-Ups: 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  • Squats: 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Plank: 3 sets x 30-60 seconds
  • Inverted Rows: 3 sets x 10-15 reps

Upper/Lower Program

Alternate between upper body and lower body days. Perform each exercise for 3 sets with 1-2 minutes rest between sets.

Upper Body Day
  • Pull-Ups: 3 sets x 5-10 reps
  • Handstand Push-Ups: 3 sets x 3-5 reps
  • Ring Rows: 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  • Dips: 3 sets x 6-10 reps
Lower Body Day
  • Squats: 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Lunges: 3 sets x 10-12 reps per leg
  • Calf Raises: 3 sets x 15-20 reps
  • Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets x 5-10 reps

Skill-Based Program

Focus on mastering specific skills. Perform each exercise for 3 sets with 1-2 minutes rest between sets.

  • Handstand Practice: 3 sets x 30-60 seconds
  • Front Lever Progressions: 3 sets x 5-10 seconds
  • Planche Practice: 3 sets x 10-20 seconds
  • One-Arm Pull-Up Progressions: 3 sets x 1-3 reps per arm

PPL (Push/Pull/Legs) Program

Rotate through push, pull, and leg days. Perform each exercise for 3 sets with 1-2 minutes rest between sets.

Push Day
  • Handstand Push-Ups: 3 sets x 3-5 reps
  • Dips: 3 sets x 6-10 reps
  • Planche Push-Ups: 3 sets x 1-3 reps
Pull Day
  • Pull-Ups: 3 sets x 5-10 reps
  • Front Lever Rows: 3 sets x 5-7 reps
  • One-Arm Rows: 3 sets x 6-8 reps per arm
Legs Day
  • Squats: 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Lunges: 3 sets x 10-12 reps per leg
  • Calf Raises: 3 sets x 15-20 reps
