Bodyweight Mastery Program

Purpose: Master full-body strength using only your bodyweight. This is a long-term, structured progression system rooted in calisthenics and gymnastic principles. Ideal for athletes seeking functional strength, control, and mobility.

Vertical Pull Progression

  1. Dead hang
  2. Scapular pulls
  3. Assisted pull-ups
  4. Negative pull-ups
  5. Chin-ups
  6. Neutral-grip pull-ups
  7. Standard pull-ups
  8. Archer pull-ups
  9. Typewriter pull-ups
  10. Chest-to-bar pull-ups
  11. Clapping pull-ups
  12. One-arm assisted pull-ups
  13. One-arm pull-up (partial ROM)
  14. Full one-arm pull-up

Form Tip: Engage your scapula first (scapular retraction) before pulling. Avoid swinging and use full range of motion.

Horizontal Pull Progression

  1. Wall rows
  2. Low incline body rows
  3. Mid incline rows
  4. Inverted rows
  5. Feet-elevated rows
  6. Wide-grip rows
  7. Archer rows
  8. One-arm rows (assisted)
  9. One-arm inverted rows
  10. Pelican rows
  11. Ice-cream makers

Form Tip: Keep your body straight and squeeze your shoulder blades at the top of each rep.

Vertical Push Progression

  1. Wall handstand hold
  2. Wall-supported handstand push-up negatives
  3. Pike push-ups
  4. Elevated pike push-ups
  5. Wall handstand push-ups
  6. Freestanding handstand hold
  7. Freestanding handstand push-up negatives
  8. Partial ROM freestanding handstand push-ups
  9. Full ROM freestanding handstand push-ups
  10. 90-degree push-ups

Form Tip: Keep your core tight and avoid flaring elbows. Lower yourself slowly with control.

Horizontal Push Progression

  1. Wall push-ups
  2. Incline push-ups
  3. Knee push-ups
  4. Standard push-ups
  5. Diamond push-ups
  6. Archer push-ups
  7. Feet-elevated push-ups
  8. Wide-grip push-ups
  9. Explosive push-ups
  10. Clapping push-ups
  11. Pseudo planche push-ups
  12. 90-degree push-ups
  13. Full planche push-ups

Form Tip: Hands under shoulders, elbows at 45°, body in straight line. Don’t rush progression.

Core Progression

  1. Plank
  2. Hollow hold
  3. Leg raises
  4. Hanging knee raises
  5. Hanging leg raises
  6. Toes to bar
  7. V-ups
  8. Dragon flags
  9. Windshield wipers
  10. L-sit hold (on floor)
  11. L-sit on parallettes or dip bars
  12. L-sit to tuck planche

Form Tip: Don’t swing. Control every movement. Breathe through the tension.

Statics Progression

  1. Wall headstand
  2. Frog stand
  3. Advanced frog stand
  4. Tuck planche
  5. Advanced tuck planche
  6. Straddle planche
  7. Full planche
  8. Tuck front lever
  9. Advanced tuck front lever
  10. Straddle front lever
  11. Full front lever
  12. Back lever
  13. Human flag (progressions)

Form Tip: Don’t rush statics. Hold each position with full body tension and perfect form.

Final Words

This program builds elite strength and control over years. Document your progress, master the basics, and never sacrifice form for speed.
