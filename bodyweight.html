Bodyweight Mastery

Where You Can Get Strong Anytime and Anywhere. Master your bodyweight through elite, scalable progressions in pulling, pushing, core, and static strength.

Vertical Pull

Vertical Pull Progression

1. Dead Hang – Hang from a bar to build grip strength.

2. Scapular Pull – Activate your upper back by pulling shoulder blades down.

3. Australian Pull-ups – Low bar rows focusing on lat engagement.

4. Negative Pull-ups – Jump up and slowly lower to strengthen the movement.

5. Band-Assisted Pull-ups – Use resistance bands to complete pull-ups.

6. Chin-ups – Underhand grip focusing on biceps and lats.

7. Pull-ups – Overhand grip pulling chest to the bar.

8. Chest-to-Bar Pull-ups – Increase the range and power.

9. Archer Pull-ups – One arm leads, the other supports.

10. One-Arm Pull-ups – Elite pulling strength, full control required.

Horizontal Pull

Horizontal Pull Progression

1. Scapular Rows – Pull shoulder blades together on low surface.

2. Inverted Rows – Pull chest to bar from horizontal position.

3. Towel Rows – Increase grip challenge by using towels.

4. Feet-Elevated Rows – More bodyweight involved.

5. Wide-Grip Rows – Hit rear delts and traps harder.

6. Close-Grip Rows – Emphasize biceps and mid-back.

7. One-Arm Rows – Isolate pulling on one side.

8. Explosive Rows – Add speed and power to movement.

9. Archer Rows – One side leads, other assists.

10. One-Arm Inverted Rows – Full-body control and raw power.

Vertical Push

Vertical Push Progression

1. Pike Push-ups – Start with hips high and push downward.

2. Elevated Pike Push-ups – Feet elevated to shift weight to shoulders.

3. Wall Handstand Hold – Kick into wall handstand and hold.

4. Wall Handstand Push-ups – Lower head to floor using arms.

5. Partial Range HSPU – Limit range to build strength slowly.

6. Box Handstand Push-ups – Legs on a box to simulate vertical push.

7. Full Handstand Push-ups – Lower from full handstand to floor and press up.

8. Deficit HSPU – Hands elevated, allowing deeper range.

9. Tiger Bend Push-ups – Advanced transition from forearms to handstand.

10. Freestanding HSPU – No wall, balance + strength combined.

Horizontal Push

Horizontal Push Progression

1. Wall Push-ups – Basic strength from standing position.

2. Knee Push-ups – Beginner-friendly horizontal push.

3. Standard Push-ups – Chest and triceps dominate.

4. Diamond Push-ups – Triceps-focused close grip.

5. Wide Push-ups – Chest and shoulders hit harder.

6. Explosive Push-ups – Add speed and plyo.

7. Archer Push-ups – One arm leads, one assists.

8. Typewriter Push-ups – Side-to-side push control.

9. One-Arm Eccentric Push-ups – Slow negative with one arm.

10. One-Arm Push-ups – Full-body tension and strength.

Core

Core Progression

1. Dead Bug – Controlled opposite arm/leg extensions.

2. Hollow Hold – Keep lower back to floor with tight abs.

3. Tuck Hold – Hold knees close to chest on bar or floor.

4. Hanging Knee Raise – Controlled lift from bar.

5. Hanging Leg Raise – Legs straight, lift to L position.

6. L-Sit – Straight legs held off ground from dip bars.

7. V-Sits – Advanced L-sit with toes to bar.

8. Hanging Windshield Wipers – Controlled oblique rotation.

9. Dragon Flags – Straight body lowered under control.

10. Front Lever – Parallel hold on bar, pure core mastery.

Statics

Static Progression

1. Wall Plank Hold – Keep tension through entire core.

2. Elbow Plank – Tight, static core brace.

3. L-Sit Hold – Legs off the ground, tight abs.

4. Tuck Planche – Small lean, knees tucked.

5. Advanced Tuck Planche – More lean, less tucked.

6. Straddle Planche – Legs wide, fully extended.

7. Full Planche – Parallel to ground, full-body tension.

8. Back Lever – Straight body, back facing ground.

9. Iron Cross Hold – Gymnast ring hold, arms wide.

10. Human Flag – Full body horizontal hold on pole.

Gear Needed

- Pull-up Bar: Essential for vertical and core training.

- Dip Bars or Parallel Bars: For dips, L-sits, and push variations.

- Resistance Bands: Helps assist or overload movements.

- Rings (optional): Adds instability and deeper control.

- Wall/Chair/Box: Used for incline or support in pushing progressions.
