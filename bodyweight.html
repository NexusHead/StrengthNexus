Exercise Progressions

Follow these progressive steps to master each movement pattern

Vertical Pull

  • Dead Hang
    Hang from a bar to build grip strength and shoulder stability. Focus on active shoulders.
  • Scapular Pull
    Activate your upper back by pulling shoulder blades down while hanging.
  • Australian Pull-ups
    Low bar rows focusing on lat engagement and proper pulling mechanics.
  • Negative Pull-ups
    Jump up and slowly lower yourself to strengthen the eccentric phase.
  • Band-Assisted Pull-ups
    Use resistance bands to complete full range pull-ups with assistance.
  • Chin-ups
    Underhand grip focusing on biceps and lats for complete upper body pull.
  • Pull-ups
    Overhand grip pulling chest to the bar with controlled movement.
  • Chest-to-Bar Pull-ups
    Increased range of motion and power development in the final phase.
  • Archer Pull-ups
    One arm leads the movement while the other provides minimal support.
  • One-Arm Pull-ups
    Elite pulling strength demonstration with full body control required.

Horizontal Pull

  • Scapular Rows
    Pull shoulder blades together on low surface to build back awareness.
  • Inverted Rows
    Pull chest to bar from horizontal position with full body alignment.
  • Towel Rows
    Increase grip challenge by using towels while performing rows.
  • Feet-Elevated Rows
    More bodyweight involved with elevated feet position for greater challenge.
  • Wide-Grip Rows
    Target rear delts and traps with wider hand positioning during rows.
  • Close-Grip Rows
    Emphasize biceps and mid-back with hands closer together during pull.
  • One-Arm Rows
    Isolate pulling strength on one side while maintaining body position.
  • Explosive Rows
    Add speed and power to the movement with controlled explosive phases.
  • Archer Rows
    One side leads the pulling motion while the other arm assists minimally.
  • One-Arm Inverted Rows
    Full-body control and raw power with single-arm horizontal pulling.

Vertical Push

  • Pike Push-ups
    Start with hips high and push downward to develop shoulder strength.
  • Elevated Pike Push-ups
    Feet elevated to shift more weight to shoulders and increase difficulty.
  • Wall Handstand Hold
    Kick into wall handstand and hold to build stability and strength.
  • Wall Handstand Push-ups
    Lower head to floor using arms while maintaining handstand position.
  • Partial Range HSPU
    Limit range of motion to build strength gradually for full movement.
  • Box Handstand Push-ups
    Legs on a box to simulate vertical push with more stability.
  • Full Handstand Push-ups
    Lower from full handstand to floor and press up with complete control.
  • Deficit HSPU
    Hands elevated on parallettes allowing deeper range of motion.
  • Tiger Bend Push-ups
    Advanced transition from forearms to handstand showing extreme control.
  • Freestanding HSPU
    No wall support, combining perfect balance with tremendous strength.

Horizontal Push

  • Wall Push-ups
    Basic strength development from standing position against wall.
  • Knee Push-ups
    Beginner-friendly horizontal push with reduced bodyweight load.
  • Standard Push-ups
    Classic chest and triceps-dominant movement with full body tension.
  • Diamond Push-ups
    Triceps-focused close grip variation with hands forming diamond shape.
  • Wide Push-ups
    Chest and front shoulders emphasized with wider hand placement.
  • Explosive Push-ups
    Add speed and plyometric power to standard push-up movement.
