Bodyweight Mastery

"Where you can get strong anywhere and anytime."

Gear

  • Pull-up Bar – For vertical and horizontal pulls
  • Dip Bars or Parallel Bars – Great for dips, L-sits, and more
  • Resistance Bands – For assisted progressions and mobility
  • Rings – To train instability and deeper muscle control
  • Yoga Mat – Comfort during core work and statics

Starter Programs

  • 3-Day Foundation Builder: Focuses on push, pull, and core with full-body circuits.
  • 4-Day Core & Control: Adds a statics and core emphasis for better control.
  • 5-Day Balanced Split: Targets different movements per day for optimal rest.
  • 6-Day Intensive: Pushes progression and skill training hard daily.
Vertical Pull Progression
  • 1. Dead Hang – Just hanging, focus on scapular engagement.
  • 2. Scapular Pulls – Small shrugs to build shoulder control.
  • 3. Assisted Pull-ups – Use bands or a chair for assistance.
  • 4. Negative Pull-ups – Slowly lower down from top position.
  • 5. Pull-ups – Full range movement.
  • 6. Chest to Bar Pull-ups – Pull higher to engage upper back.
  • 7. Archer Pull-ups – Isolate one side at a time.
  • 8. Typewriter Pull-ups – Pull across left to right at the top.
  • 9. One-arm Assisted Pull-ups – Assist with band or fingers.
  • 10. One-arm Pull-ups – Elite level vertical pulling.
Horizontal Pull Progression
  • 1. Ring Rows – Adjustable and perfect for beginners.
  • 2. Body Rows – Under a bar with feet grounded.
  • 3. Feet Elevated Rows – Increase difficulty.
  • 4. Wide Grip Rows – Focus on rear delts.
  • 5. Archer Rows – Isolates each side for strength.
  • 6. Towel Rows – Grip and upper back challenge.
  • 7. L-Sit Rows – Core + horizontal strength.
  • 8. Inverted Rows – Advanced horizontal pull variant.
  • 9. One-arm Rows with Assist – Build to single arm.
  • 10. One-arm Horizontal Rows – High level move.
Vertical Push Progression
  • 1. Wall Plank Hold – Start pressing against gravity.
  • 2. Pike Push-ups – Mimics overhead pressing.
  • 3. Elevated Pike Push-ups – Greater angle = harder.
  • 4. Wall Assisted HS Holds – Balance and wrist strength.
  • 5. Wall Handstand Push-ups (Negatives) – Eccentric control.
  • 6. Wall Handstand Push-ups – Full pressing.
  • 7. Freestanding HS Holds – Static balance challenge.
  • 8. Freestanding HSPUs – Strength + balance.
  • 9. Tiger Bend Push-ups – Triceps + control.
  • 10. 90 Degree Push-ups – Elite vertical pushing.
Horizontal Push Progression
  • 1. Wall Push-ups – First pushup variation for absolute beginners.
  • 2. Incline Push-ups – Slight angle eases the load.
  • 3. Knee Push-ups – Basic press with less weight.
  • 4. Full Push-ups – Standard ground push.
  • 5. Diamond Push-ups – Triceps and inner chest.
  • 6. Wide Push-ups – Outer chest focus.
  • 7. Archer Push-ups – Move side to side, single-arm prep.
  • 8. Typewriter Push-ups – Control across horizontal axis.
  • 9. One-arm Assisted Push-ups – Band or surface assist.
  • 10. One-arm Push-ups – True mastery of bodyweight push.
Core Progression
  • 1. Deadbugs – Coordination and control.
  • 2. Hollow Body Hold – Core engagement baseline.
  • 3. Leg Raises – Lower core activation.
  • 4. Tucked L-sits – Balance + core tightness.
  • 5. V-ups – Dynamic control of core.
  • 6. Hanging Leg Raises – More difficulty from bar.
  • 7. L-sits – Static strength and endurance.
  • 8. Hanging Windshield Wipers – Obliques + control.
  • 9. Dragon Flags – Posterior chain + core elite move.
  • 10. Front Lever Progression – Advanced static core challenge.
Static Mastery
  • 1. Wall HS Hold – Handstand positioning.
  • 2. Frog Stand – Start of planche pathway.
  • 3. Tuck Planche – Core and shoulder strength.
  • 4. Advanced Tuck – More load and balance.
  • 5. Straddle Planche – Spreads load over wider base.
  • 6. Full Planche – Ultimate pushing static hold.
  • 7. Front Lever Tuck – Build lat and core tension.
  • 8. Advanced Front Lever – Legs extended slightly.
  • 9. Full Front Lever – Horizontal pull mastery.
  • 10. Iron Cross / Human Flag – Rare static feats of control.
