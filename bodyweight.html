Exercise Progressions

Vertical Pull
  • 1. Dead Hang – Develop grip and decompress shoulders.
  • 2. Scapular Pulls – Activate lats and prep for pulls.
  • 3. Australian Pull-up – Horizontal pulling base.
  • 4. Jumping Pull-up – Build confidence and strength.
  • 5. Chin-up – Focused bicep engagement.
  • 6. Pull-up – True vertical pull mastery starts here.
  • 7. L-sit Pull-up – Adds core control.
  • 8. Archer Pull-up – One arm strength builder.
  • 9. Typewriter Pull-up – Build unilateral endurance.
  • 10. One Arm Pull-up – Advanced peak of vertical strength.
Horizontal Pull
  • 1. Wall Rows – Beginner-friendly horizontal pull.
  • 2. Desk Rows – Accessible at home.
  • 3. Ring Rows – Improve scap control.
  • 4. Inverted Rows – Mid-back emphasis.
  • 5. Wide Rows – Increase scapular retraction.
  • 6. Feet-Elevated Rows – Add difficulty.
  • 7. L-sit Rows – Challenge control and core.
  • 8. Front Lever Pulls – Static + dynamic.
  • 9. One Arm Rows – Raw unilateral power.
  • 10. Full Front Lever – Masterclass in body tension.
